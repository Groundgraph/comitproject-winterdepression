Winter Depression

Understanding Seasonal Affective Disorder

What is Winter Depression?

Winter Depression is a form of SAD, characterized by mood changes and symptoms that occur during the winter months. Denmark is ranked number 9 according to a study conducted by the wellness pulse organization. This is due to the long, dark winters and reduced sunlight exposure. These are concerning numbers, that is why we have taken it upon ourselves to raise awareness and provide information on this condition.

Symptoms & Effects

Signs and symptoms of SAD may include:

  • Feeling listless, sad or down most of the day, nearly every day
  • Losing interest in activities you once enjoyed
  • Having low energy and feeling sluggish
  • Having problems with sleeping too much
  • Experiencing carbohydrate cravings, overeating and weight gain
  • Having difficulty concentrating
  • Feeling hopeless, worthless or guilty
  • Having thoughts of not wanting to live

If you or someone you know is experiencing these symptoms, it is vital you seek professional help or some other form of support.

Who is Affected?

  • Gender: Usually women are more affected than men.
  • Age: Young adults are more likely to experience SAD, from teens to people in their 20s.
  • Location: People living far from the equator are at higher risk due to reduced sunlight exposure during winter months.
  • Other Factors: A family history of SAD or other forms of depression can increase the risk. Vitamin D deficiency is also linked to higher rates of SAD.

Finding Light in Winter

Solutions & Courses of Action

Light Therapy

Light Therapy is a treatment that involves exposure to artificial light to make up for the lack of natural sunlight during winter months. It helps regulate mood and reduce symptoms of Seasonal Affective Disorder. Examples of such therapy include light boxes and lamps designed to mimic natural sunlight. It is also vital that before you start such a treatment, you consult with a healthcare professional to ensure it is appropriate for your specific condition.

Learn more about light therapy:

  • Depressionsforeningen - Light Therapy Treatment for Winter Depression

Lifestyle Changes

Dealing with winter depression can also be done through simple lifestyle changes such as:

  • Stay out and in light
  • Do Physical Activity
  • Brighten up everything (draw curtains, brighter lights)
  • Be Near Windows
  • Take in as much sunlight as possible

Professional Help

Seeking professional help is important if symptoms of winter depression persist. Mental health professionals can provide therapy, counseling, and medication if necessary. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) has been shown to be effective in treating SAD. It is essential to reach out to a healthcare provider to discuss symptoms and explore treatment options tailored to individual needs. Another form of medication that might work for you is to take Vitamin D supplements. These can help alleviate symptoms related to vitamin D deficiency, which is common during the darker months.
